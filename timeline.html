Timeline

  • 1653

    The Dutch erect a fortifed barrier to protect lower Manhattan from attack. This wall ran from the Hudson River to the East River along what is now called Wall Street. Citizens threw their garbage over the wall, causing run off into the rivers. This was the first of many future risks to the oyster populations health.

  • 1685

    The wall was taken down by the British revealing a huge garbage dump. The dump extended to a small pond, called the Collect. It was the only part of the island were the slaughting of animals was allowed. Tanneries and other polluting industries set up shop here.
