Timeline

  • 1609

    Hudson sailed into New York.

  • 1679

    Conservation measures enacted in NY and NJ to preserve natural oyster beds and prevent overfishing. Brookhaven, LI limited the number of fishing vessels to 10 in Great South Bay between Fire Island and Long Island.

  • 1715

    Oystering banned during spawning season - May 1 to September 1. In addition, oyster beds zoned and restricted to local residents. Non-residents caught harvesting were subject to gear seizure and fines.

  • 1820

    Natural oyster beds in NY and NJ depleted. Oyster cultivation began. Beds replanted with oysters grown elsewhere. Spats or seed oysters from Chesapeake used.

  • 1880-1920

    The golden boom of oysters in New York. 700 million oysters produced a year.

  • 1910

    600 million gallons of untreated sewage dumped into New York waterways everyday.

  • 1920

    New York oyster beds closed following cholera outbreaks from sewage.

  • 1930

    Fishing no longer possible due to quality of NYC waters.

  • 1972

    Clean Water Act passed giving a deadline of 1985 for all bodies of water to be swimmable and fishable.

  • 2017

    To date the Billion Oyster Project has planted 24 million oysters in New York's harbor.

  • 2035

    The Billion Oyster Project's goal is to have planted 2 billion oysters in New York's harbor.
